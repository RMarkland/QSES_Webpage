QSES Members

Meet the QSES team.

QSES is led by graduate students working across quantum science, engineering, materials, devices, policy, outreach, and professional development.

Home Members Events Funding

Matt Whalen

President

Originally from Mansfield, Massachusetts, Matt earned his bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering from the University of Delaware. He is now in his third year of the Ph.D. program in Quantum Science and Engineering at UD. Outside of research, Matt enjoys staying active, spending time with friends, and playing chess.

Lan Hoang Mai

Vice President

Lan earned his Bachelor of Arts in Math and Physics from McDaniel College. He is a PhD Candidate in Quantum Science and Engineering and a research assistant in Dr. Matt Doty's group, working on photonic devices on III-V semiconductor materials for quantum technology applications.

Jackson Hanle

Secretary

Jackson is a QSE student at UD researching molecular beam epitaxy-grown oxichalcogenides. Jackson studied physics as an undergraduate, doing research in defect identification in III-V semiconductors, and enjoys music, poetry, and trying new recipes.

Brandon Bauer

Treasurer

Brandon is a Ph.D. student in Quantum Science and Engineering researching two-dimensional materials. He earned degrees in Mathematics and Physics, and in Computer and Data Science, from Lebanon Valley College in May 2024.

Radheya Sarode

Outreach Coordinator

Radheya is a third-year QSE graduate student whose research focuses on computational techniques for designing nanoscale dielectric structures that enhance and collect quantum emissions from quantum-optical interfaces.

Reid Markland

Social Media Director

Reid earned bachelor's degrees in physics and mathematics from Auburn University. He works in Dr. Zhao's group on low-temperature measurements of quantum Hall states and charge transport in topological devices built in-house.

Youssef El Gharably

Professional Development Director

Youssef is a first-year PhD student in Quantum Science and Engineering on the Quantum Nanotechnology track. He is part of the FINCH research group led by Dr. Ryan Comes and is interested in exotic quantum materials for better qubits.

Shedrack Dafe

Graduate Student Government Representative

Shedrack is a PhD Candidate in Quantum Science and Engineering focused on device fabrication, GeSn growth using MBE, and material characterization of SiGeSn for electronics, infrared optoelectronics, and quantum technologies.

Ranjan Mahanth

Social Chair

Ranjan is a first-year QSE PhD student on the Theory track. He earned BS and MS degrees in Materials Science and Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University and works in Dr. Swati Singh's group on quantum superfluid behavior.
